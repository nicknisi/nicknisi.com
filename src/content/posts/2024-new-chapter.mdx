Big news - after a year at Meta, I'm making a move that I'm really excited about. I'm joining [WorkOS](https://workos.com) as a Developer Experience Engineer! 🎉

## The Meta Chapter

For the past year, I've been working with an amazing team on Meta's Ads Manager, specifically - the Optimal Account Setup team. There's something pretty special about working on the app where React was born - you can feel the history in every component. The Ads Manager is literally the oldest and largest React application in the world, which comes with both impressive legacy and interesting engineering challenges. I've had the opportunity to ship meaningful features that help advertisers onboard and businesses make the most of their advertising experience, working alongside talented engineers and learning how engineering operates at massive scale. The internal tooling at Meta is legitimately impressive, and getting to work with these tools while contributing to such a historically significant app has been a unique experience.

![My time at Meta running ads on Instagram](@/assets/posts/meta-ad.png)

One of my favorite memories was testing some new ad features and having several friends message me like "Dude, why am I seeing your face in my Instagram feed?" Hey, someone's gotta test these features, right? At least I can say I've been both an ads engineer AND an Instagram model!

## Time for a New Adventure

As engineers, we're constantly growing and learning what environments help us do our best work. While my time at Meta has been filled with valuable experiences and fantastic teammates, I found myself drawn to opportunities that would let me work more directly with developers and combine my passion for engineering with developer education.

When the opportunity at WorkOS came up, it felt like one of those "aha!" moments. As a Developer Experience Engineer, I'll get to combine what I love most: diving deep into technical problems, working with developers, and teaching/sharing knowledge. It's a role that lets me be hands-on with code while helping other developers succeed.

## 5 Things that Have Me Excited

I'm particularly excited about:

1. Getting back to more hands-on engineering work
2. Expanding my technical toolkit across different languages and tools
3. Working directly with developers to solve real problems
4. Creating content and teaching (you know I love that!)
5. Building something meaningful in the developer tools space

## Looking Forward

This move feels like a natural evolution in my journey. While Meta gave me valuable insights into large-scale engineering operations, WorkOS offers an opportunity to have direct impact in the developer tools ecosystem. It's a chance to help build something that makes developers' lives easier - and if you know me, you know that's right up my alley!

Stay tuned for more updates as I dive into this new adventure. And hey, if you're working with WorkOS or curious about it, hit me up - I'd love to chat!
